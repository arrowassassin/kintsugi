THE TOUR

What Aegis does, in plain English. No Rust knowledge required. Scroll through six scenes — each one is a small thing happening on your computer that Aegis is in the middle of.

↓ press any arrow key to skim, or just scroll

> 01

An AI is now typing for you

AI coding tools don't suggest commands anymore — they run them. Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, Gemini, your own scripts. They open files, install packages, push branches, drop databases.

Most of the time that's fine. Every now and then, the wrong line slips through and something you cared about is gone before you can react. The window between "hm" and "oh no" is shorter than a blink.

> 02

Aegis sits between the AI and your machine

Aegis isn't a plug-in for one tool. It sits at the process layer — the place every program on your computer eventually has to pass through.

It hooks into the agents that support hooks, an MCP server for the ones that speak it, and a $PATH shim that catches the rest. The agent doesn't have to cooperate. Even a raw shell call gets caught.

> 03

Rules decide, not a guessing AI

Whether a command can hurt you is decided by deterministic rules a human wrote — not by an AI making a probabilistic guess.

This matters. An AI deciding "should I let this run?" can be argued past — prompt-injected, tricked, talked down to "actually it's fine." Rules can't. rm -rf / is always blocked. Always. There's no override.

A local model is allowed to add caution (escalate the amber middle) — never to remove it.

> 04

Anything destructive is reversible

Before any destructive command runs, Aegis quietly takes a copy-on-write snapshot of the files it's about to touch. It's nearly free on modern filesystems — you don't notice.

If you don't like what happened, type aegis undo. The files come back. A filesystem watcher catches things that slipped past the snapshot, as a backstop.

The honest guarantee is "nothing is unrecoverable" — not "nothing runs un-warned." That's a harder problem; we don't claim to solve it.

> 05

A log nobody can quietly edit

Every command, every decision, every approval — written to a small local file. Each entry includes a hash of the one before it.

If someone (or something) edits an old entry to cover their tracks, its hash changes and every entry after it stops matching. Tampering isn't impossible — but it's visible. You can see the chain break.

It's not a blockchain. There's no consensus, no mining, no token. It's just one file with linked hashes — the same trick Git uses for commits.

> 06

Nothing leaves your machine

There's no cloud. No account. No telemetry. The decision happens on your computer; the log lives on your disk; the optional model runs offline.

The only network call Aegis ever makes is the one-time download of that local model, and it's checksummed before it runs. Your source code, your commands, your secrets — never transmitted.

> END

That's the whole pitch

Move fast with AI tools. Nothing is unrecoverable. The thing that can hurt you doesn't get to surprise you.
